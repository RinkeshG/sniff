01 · The Idea

The friend who read every label.

Sniff takes a confusing cat food label and makes it clear — what's actually in it, what's missing, and what to do about it.

Not a magazine (too distant). Not a lab (too cold). Not a SaaS (too transactional). Sniff is a knowledgeable friend with no commercial stake who tells you what's what. Warm, observant, slightly dry, never anxious, never preachy.

The core moment: confusion → clarity. "Crude protein 30% min" means nothing to a cat parent. "33% on dry matter, adequate but below what a mouse provides" means everything. The visual system exists to make that transition feel fast, trustworthy, and complete.

Your cat is probably fine. The food is probably fine. Here is what's actually going on. Decide.

02 · The Mark

Just the word, dotted in ginger.

No icon, no mascot lock-up. The logotype is sniff — lowercase, set in Young Serif — with the i dotted by a single ginger dot. That dot is the one spot of colour, and it does all the work.

snıff

Cream on dark · Charcoal on light · the dot stays ginger on both. (It blinks — watch the i.)

The dot

Sized to Young Serif's own tittle (about 0.16em) and set where the typeface's dot would sit — so it reads as type, not a sticker stuck on top. Ginger, #E08A3A, always.

The blink

Every five or six seconds the dot closes to a line and opens again, in place. A slow cat blink — the wordmark's one moving part. It's affection, not a notification: it never speeds up and never repeats in a burst. Below ~64px, or anywhere static (print, favicons), drop it and keep the dot solid.

Clear space & colour

Keep clear space of one n on every side. The wordmark is Charcoal on light and Cream on dark; the dot is Ginger on both. Never fill the letters with ginger, and never recolour the dot.

Not Sniff
Ginger letters. A drawn cat-face icon bolted on. The dot dropped, recoloured, or squeezed into the stem.
The dot is the only colour — and it stays a round dot, above the i.
Sniff

Let the one moving part be the whole personality.

Beyond the wordmark, a kit of cat marks — loaders, empty states, reactions, badges — extends the brand across the product. Those live in the design-system reference, not here.

05 · The Product, Rendered

What a Sniff looks like.

The analysis card is the product. Everything else — landing page, social, marketing — exists to bring someone to this card. Get this right, everything else follows.

Fixed anatomy

Every Sniff card uses the same layout, same order, same colour meanings. After three uses, a user scans instead of reads.

01 · Header

Brand, product name, price per 100g, daily cost for a 4kg cat.

02 · Three headline findings

The most important number (Checks Out or Worth a Sniff), the most concerning number, and the most notable gap (Gone Missing).

03 · Position bar

Where the key metric sits between AAFCO minimum and prey-matched ideal. The dot IS the number in context.

04 · First ingredient

Named, set large in serif. What your cat eats most of, given visual weight.

05 · Gone Missing

Every non-disclosed field, rendered as dashed Stone boxes. The absence is visible.

06 · Voice

How the friend talks.

Lead with the fact. Hedge after, not before. The voice is in the verbs, not the adjectives.

Not Sniff
"Give your feline the nutrition they truly deserve."
Performative care. Nobody talks like this.
Sniff
"Hits the protein target. Fibre is high — your cat might poop more."
Specific. Useful. Quietly funny.

Surface copy

Hero: "Read the back of the bag. We did."

Empty state: "Pick a food. We'll show you the math."

404: "Page went somewhere we can't find. Like a sock."

Footer: "Made by one person in Bangalore who got tired of guessing."

07 · Never

What Sniff doesn't do.

If you catch yourself doing any of these, stop. This list keeps the brand the brand.

Voice

  • Issue a score. No 8/10. No A+. "Good" depends on the cat.
  • Use: premium, best, trusted, leading, AI-powered.
  • Use cat puns: purr-fect, fur baby, pawsome, meow-velous.
  • Manufacture urgency. No countdowns.
  • Lecture. No "Did you know…"

Visual

  • Use colour decoratively. Every colour has a semantic reason.
  • Use Fraunces, Playfair, Cormorant, DM Serif, Instrument Serif for display.
  • Use Inter, Geist, Manrope, Space Grotesk for body.
  • Use stock cat photography or AI-generated illustration.
  • Use gradients, glassmorphism, glow, or dot-grid backgrounds.
  • Reorder the analysis card anatomy.

Product

  • Sell. No "shop now." Referral links plainly labelled.
  • Gate analysis behind sign-up.
  • Auto-play anything.
  • Add a feature without removing one.
08 · Typography

Young Serif + Sora.

Young Serif is warm, slightly imperfect, like hand-set type. Sora is geometric but has character — clean without being clinical. This pair feels like "a smart friend made this."

Display — Young Serif

Cereals are the first ingredient.

Used for: page titles, The Nose Knows verdict, ingredient #1 callout, section heads, asides. The friend's voice in type.

Body — Sora

For an adult indoor cat at four kilos: about 55g a day, split into two meals. Costs ₹33 a day, roughly a thousand a month. Adequate baseline nutrition but the brand declines to name the meat or disclose taurine.

Used for: body text, data, UI labels, buttons, meta information.

Numbers — Sora 800

39%

Numbers in Sora Extra Bold, tabular figures. The number does the persuading; the context comes after.
